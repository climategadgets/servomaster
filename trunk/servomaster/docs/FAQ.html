ServoMaster: FAQ

What is a project status?
Architecture is clear, design is done, Java implementation for one of the controllers (FT639) is done. Second is coming. Stay tuned.

Why Java?
First of all, this is my current language of choice. Second, it is good for rapid prototyping. Third, and probably the most important, despite all its drawbacks, it is really platform independent. This last one matters a lot, at least to me.

What about C?
No C. C++ port, though, is planned. Don't ask about the timeframe. If you want to help, you're welcome.

Why FerretTronics is the only implementation avaialable?
Because it was the cheapest thing available at the time, almost half as expensive as the next one. Is it the best? No, here's why. Is it the worst? Don't think so. It allows to do things other controllers don't (header length feature). The protocol is not bad either.

Are there other hardware controller drivers coming?
You bet. The next one is probably SSC, unless I get to implement my crazy ideas sooner.

I've bought the FT639 and put it together, and it doesn't work, what the hell?
The picture at the FerretTronics FAQ page is ambiguous. It looks like a back of DB-9 female connector, while in reality it is a male connector. The signal goes to pin 3, the ground goes to pin 5.

If this fails, check if the signal level on the chip input is high enough. I had to short the 20K resistor to make it work with my Asus P-2B onboard serial controller (well, that was before I sorted out the confusion with the pinout, I'm surprised it worked at all... Hope I didn't bust my serial port...)

What is this "silent mode" thing?
If you are familiar with the servos, you probably know that they can make a sound that can make you jump, especially caught off guard. The application this project is serving is intended to have the servos operate next to people whose comfort level is paramount, so they must not be scared do death periodically when the servos come to life.

As a side effect, this reduces the energy consumption and servo wear. Since the servos may be working 24x7, this may be important as well.
